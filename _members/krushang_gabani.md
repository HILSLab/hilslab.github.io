I’m a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering with a focus on robotics, physics-based simulation, and contact-rich interaction. My work centers on understanding how robots can reliably interact with the physical world, especially in the presence of compliance, uncertainty, and complex contact dynamics.
I believe the next frontier in engineering lies in distributed, embodied intelligence : bringing together learning systems and physical agents to solve complex, real-world problems at scale.
I’ve been developing scalable, physically grounded simulation environments for nonlinear deformable body interaction, enabling high-fidelity sim-to-real transfer. The goal is to create systems where robots can be trained, validated, and deployed with strong alignment to real world physics.
I enjoy working across both simulation and real systems, from modeling complex interactions to integrating perception and control on robotic platforms.
